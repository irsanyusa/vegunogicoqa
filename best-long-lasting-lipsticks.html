Best Long Lasting Lipsticks for Every Style and Occasion

Publish date: 2024-07-02

The right shade of lipstick can elevate any outfit, transforming a casual look into something intentional and put together. Depending on the shade and finish, lipstick can brighten up your face, add color to a neutral clothing palette or make a bold statement. But this can all be undermined if your makeup wears off soon after you’ve put it on. On top of that, it can fade unevenly for a messy, undesirable effect.

Long-lasting lipstick is designed to overcome the usual wear-and-tear of lip makeup. Historically, these products have compensated for their staying power with drying formulas that can be uncomfortable to wear for long periods of time. Today’s top selections are reliable for hours of wear, while keeping lips hydrated and soft. Available in a range of picture-perfect shades, we’ve compiled this list of our favorite long-lasting lipsticks to help you find the right one for you.

Our top picks for the best long-lasting lipsticks

The best long-lasting lipsticks should stay in place for hours, even after eating and drinking. Beyond that, they may not appear alike at all! Just like with a regular lipstick, you can choose between shiny, satin, and matte finishes, as well as from nudes to brights. There’s also a winner for every level of budget.

What should you look for when buying long-lasting lipstick?

Every pick on this list is a winner, but some will probably be a better fit for you and your needs than others. To determine which lipstick is the right one for you, it can be helpful to consider these key questions:

How much do I want to spend?

Our selections span from $5.49 to $45, so there really is something for everyone. When deciding how much you want to budget for this purchase, you may want to think about how often you will use the product and therefore what is worth it to you. If you have lots of other lipsticks in your rotation—or if you’re not a regular lipstick wearer in general—you may want to spend less on this individual item. If you know you’re going to use this as a daily or weekly workhorse product, then it may be worth it to upgrade to a slightly more expensive pick.

We back every choice on this list, but the more expensive options do often come with more hydrating formulas and greater color selection. You also may be paying for more fancy packaging, so consider if that is important to you. Brand loyalists may be willing to pay more to stay with their preferred brand, while others may be more open to trying something new.

What finish do I prefer?

The majority of options in the long-lasting lipstick space come in a matte finish, which is a fun, modern style that can be dressed up or down. However, you may prefer something more slick and shiny, in which case the NYX Shine Loud High Shine Lip Color may be a better fit. If you’re somewhere in between, a satin finish like Armani Beauty Lip Power Satin Long Lasting Lipstick is a great middle ground. For something more subtle, Stila’s selection of shimmers and sheers are a pretty, but low-key option.

Do I often experience dry lips?

The one downside of long-lasting lipstick is that hours of coverage can lead to dehydration of the lips, which in turn can make the skin scaly or flaky. While all brands recommend lightly exfoliating and moisturizing lips before lipstick application, some formulas are more hydrating than others. If dry lips are something you are prone to, make sure to look out for ingredients like vitamin E, avocado oil or other oils, or other extracts that tout hydrating properties.

How we chose the best long-lasting lipsticks

To ensure every lipstick on this list delivered on expectations, we conducted our own review process and also scoured the internet for the most high-performing products in the market. Lipsticks were worn for at least 8 hours and tested to see if they held up during eating and drinking, then assessed for pigmentation and feel.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What’s the best way to apply long-lasting lipstick?

Many of these lipsticks are in liquid form, meaning they’re applied with a separate applicator first dipped into the liquid, then placed on lips. Different brands have different-shaped applicators designed for more precise movement or coverage. The bullet-shaped products can be applied just like any regular lipstick: either directly from the bullet, or tapped onto the lips with a finger or brush.

For the most long-lasting, smooth effect, it’s recommended to lightly exfoliate and then moisturize the lips before applying any lipstick. This removes any dry skin that may drag or stick to the product and ensures that lips are hydrated before they are sealed under the formula. To help the lipstick stay in place, blot the lips lightly after moisturizing to remove any excess that may encourage sliding or smudging.

How can I choose the right lipstick shade for me?

When deciding what color will look best on you, you’ll want to take into account your hair color, skin tone, and eye color so you don’t pick a shade that clashes with your natural coloring. Knowing your skin’s undertone (cool, warm, or neutral) will also help guide you when buying eyeshadow palettes, blush, and even tinted moisturizers or tinted sunscreens (which tend to go by undertone rather than a wide range of shades). Skin is cool-toned if there is a blue, pink or red flatter your skin; if you look better in silver than gold jewelry; and if your veins look more blue in color. Correspondingly, cool-toned people look better with cooler-toned nudes and colors with a blue or purple undertone, such as a cherry red rather than a brick red.

Warm-toned skin will show up as a yellow, gold, or olive hint to skin; with veins that are more green than blue; and as better-suited to gold than silver jewelry. Warm-toned nudes will be a better fit, as well as colors with an orange or red undertone, such as corals, terracotta, brick red etc. Neutral-toned skin is best suited by a range of colors, so it can be best to focus on how light or dark your skin is to find a complementary color—but really anything can work!

What’s the best way to remove long-lasting lipstick?

A downside of long-lasting lipstick is that it can be a pain to remove, thanks to its staying power. You likely won’t be able to just wipe it off with a tissue, so leverage your skincare and deploy a cleanser for comprehensive removal. Oil-based cleansers are often preferred, as they’re better able to lift the product from the skin and hydrate lips in the process. Try not to rub too hard or pull at the skin, instead use gentle movements, warm water, and sufficient cleanser to take any lipstick off.

Which lipstick stays even after eating?

Each brand claims a different amount of wear time, from eight hours to 16+ hours, but in general you’re looking at a full eight-hour workday of wear (and most likely longer). This includes eating. Eating and drinking will cause more wear and tear and may reduce the overall wear time, but all of our choices hold up to at least one meal and a few drinks. Keep the product with you, just in case you want to reapply, but rest assured, you’ve got several hours of wear guaranteed.
