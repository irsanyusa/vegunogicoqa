The Voice is back for Season 25, with returning coaches John Legend, Reba McEntire, and Chance the Rapper, as well as the show’s first coaching duo Dan + Shay. A new report reveals the salaries these coaches are apparently making for their work on the show.

What Are the Coaches’ Salaries on The Voice Season 25?

According to The Sun, NBC had planned to “tighten spending,” which could affect the coaches’ salaries. In a new report from the outlet, a source reveals the coaches’ alleged paychecks for Season 25 of the singing competition.

The source shared that McEntire, Legend, and Dan + Shay are getting paid $13 million for this season, which they described as “the higher end of the fee range for talent” on the network. Chance the Rapper, on the other hand, is reportedly making $8 million.

The Sun compared these figures to past coach salaries, pointing out that Shelton previously made $13 million per season when he was a coach, as did his wife Gwen Stefani. Coaches Niall Horan and Camila Cabello reportedly earned the $8 million sum.

The highest-paid coaches throughout the series include Kelly Clarkson — who allegedly made $15 million — and Ariana Grande, who was reported to have made between $20 million and $25 million for her season.

The New Season Is Currently Airing

The new season of The Voice is currently airing on NBC, as the coaches hone their teams before the live shows kick off. That includes pitting their artists against each other in the Battles and Knockouts.

Three of the season’s coaches have appeared on the show before. Legend is the longest-running coach on the panel, having appeared in nine seasons. McEntire got her start last season, replacing longtime coach Blake Shelton. Chance the Rapper is returning for a second season after making his debut in Season 23.

Dan + Shay are newcomers this season, as the show introduced its very first coaching duo. The country artists share the responsibility of choosing singers for their team and mentoring the contestants before their performances.
