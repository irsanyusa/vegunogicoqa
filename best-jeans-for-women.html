Best Jeans For Women: 8 Pairs Fashion Experts Swear By

Publish date: 2024-05-17

Finding a great-fitting pair of jeans is like hitting the jackpot. There’s no shortage of options, and your perfect pair largely depends on your body shape, height, personal style preferences, and budget, of course. The same pair of jeans your BFF loves and looks killer in may fit awkwardly on you. The best jeans for women should be comfortable, without digging into your waist or giving you the dreaded denim camel toe. They should also fit properly in the butt, thighs, and calves, hit at the right length, and just generally make you feel fabulous. Whether you’re on the hunt for the best baggy denim that’s on trend or want a great, go-to pair of high-waisted skinny jeans to elongate your legs, we’ve rounded up our favorites for every body, budget, and style.

How to choose the best jeans for women

The perfect pair of denim does exist. You just have to know how to find it. We spoke to celebrity fashion stylist Kim Appelt, author of Style for EveryBODY, to get the lowdown on finding those fit-like-a-glove dream jeans you’ll never want to take off.

Body type

“This is perhaps the single most important factor to consider when looking for jeans,” Appelt says. “The ideal jeans accentuate your personal silhouette and body type. Flattering styles exist for every body, from the sleek and statuesque to the curvaceous and petite.” Take note of the styles, cuts, washes and rises you gravitate toward and what’s already in your closet that you love. This will help narrow down what looks and feels best on your body.

Fabric and stretch

According to Appelt, fabric is the second most important factor to look at when picking the best jeans for women. “Be thoughtful looking for the perfect blend of comfort and structure, with a hint of stretch.”

Style

“Personal style knows no bounds, with options ranging from classic straight legs to the trendiest flares, wide legs, and skinnies.” Appelt suggests selecting a style that mirrors your personal aesthetic and looks best on your body shape.

Rise

Low-rise jeans may be back in fashion, but while it’s fun to try trends, Appelt recommends picking a rise (high, mid or low) that you feel most comfortable in and that flatters your shape. Try on jeans in different rises to discover what works for you.

Color and wash

In general, darker denim tends to be more slimming and can look dressier for going out—or as a great alternative to dress pants for the office. Lighter, distressed washes are a bit more relaxed and lean more casual. It all depends on the vibe you’re after—and where you’re headed. Note: Darker dyes tends to transfer, so you may want to give your jeans a few washes before wearing with your favorite white sneakers or accessorizing with a designer handbag.

Budget

Less is more when it comes to the quantity, not the quality of your jeans. “Your budget and intention matter,” says Appelt. “It's important to understand that investing in quality denim is a wise decision, as premium jeans endure and age gracefully.”

Brand and fit

“Different brands have distinct fits and sizing,” notes Appelt. “Exploration and experimentation are part of the journey to uncover your favored label. Do some research before you head to the store on the different fits then take your time trying as many brands on that you can so you have a better understanding of what works for you.” Also, knowing how to measure your waist for pants properly can help you navigate each brand’s size charts, which may differ quite a bit.

How do I choose the right jeans for my body shape?

Cannon suggests starting with what you love about the favorite jeans you own right now. “What style, color, wash, and fit do you like best? Then research on which brands have the things you like best about denim.” He also advises going to a big department store like Bloomingdales, which carries a wide variety of brands, so you can try different brands, sizes and styles all at once. “Ask the sales team to tell you their customer favorites and which brand and fit may be best for your body.”

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What jeans should I wear if I have a belly?

If you're looking to flatter your midsection, Appelt advises opting for high-rise jeans with some stretch. “These jeans provide support and create a smooth silhouette. Dark washes are excellent for a slimming effect and you may want to consider styles with a wider waistband or tummy control panels for added comfort,” she says.

Should you go by waist or hips for jeans?

“When choosing jeans, it's generally advisable to consider both your waist and hips measurements,” says Appelt. “The best fit often aligns with your larger measurement, whether it's the waist or hips. Some jeans may fit more comfortably around your waist, while others accommodate your hip measurement. Ultimately, the perfect fit is a balance between the two, ensuring comfort and style.”

How can you make jeans look classy?

Jen Reed, influencer, shopping expert and founder of @TheSisterStudioIG, recommends taking a pair of non-distressed, medium-wash denim and pairing them with a crisp, polished white button down shirt. “Add a slingback heel, a loafer or even a boot, and add your favorite jewelry for a look that's classy, chic, and works for any occasion,” she advises. “Throw a blazer over your shoulders for an extra-polished look.”

How do I know which rise works best for me?

According to Cannon, “it’s all about how the jean makes you feel. The mirror is your best friend.”

He tells his clients to have a second mirror at home to pull out and see what you look like from behind. Appelt adds that short-waisted women will generally pull off a lower rise, while those with long torsos can rock a high rise.

How can I tell if I’m wearing the right jeans size?

“If your jeans feel too tight, they’ll look too tight,” says Cannon. “And if you feel uncomfortable, you’ll look uncomfortable.” The best jeans for women should fit comfortably and be easy to wear, walk and sit down in. Once you find a style you love, he recommends stocking up in different colors and maybe buying a backup.
