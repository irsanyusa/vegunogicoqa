Kathy Bates (Actress) - On This Day

Publish date: 2024-06-09

Profession: Actress

Biography: Kathy Bates is an American actress who has won an Academy Award, two Primetime Emmy Awards, and two Golden Globe Awards. She is best known for her breakthrough role as the obsessive fan Annie Wilkes in the 1990 thriller "Misery," for which she won the Oscar for Best Actress.

Bates was born in Memphis, Tennessee, and studied theater at Southern Methodist University. She moved to New York City in 1970 to pursue an acting career, initially struggling to find work but eventually earning a Tony Award nomination for "'night, Mother" in 1983.

Her portrayal of Annie Wilkes in "Misery" (1990) established her as a versatile character actress. Other notable films include "Fried Green Tomatoes" (1991), "Dolores Claiborne" (1995), "Titanic" (1997), "Primary Colors" (1998), "About Schmidt" (2002), "Revolutionary Road" (2008), "The Blind Side" (2009), and "Midnight in Paris" (2011).

On television, Bates won Primetime Emmy Awards for guest roles on "Two and a Half Men" (2012) and "American Horror Story: Coven" (2013), with further nominations for roles in shows like "Six Feet Under" and "Harry's Law".

Bates has battled ovarian cancer since 2003 and breast cancer since 2012, becoming an activist and spokesperson for lymphedema awareness after her double mastectomy.

Throughout her career, she has received numerous accolades, including being nominated for the Triple Crown of Acting, and has worked with acclaimed directors like Steven Spielberg, James Cameron, Clint Eastwood, and Woody Allen.

Now in her 70s, Bates continues to act in films and television shows, with recent projects including "The Miracle Club" (2023) and "Are You There God? It's Me, Margaret." (2023).
